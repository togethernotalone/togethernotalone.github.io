Resources

If you are ever in need of urgent assistance, please refer to the contacts below.
Remember, help is just a phone call away. You deserve a bright future.

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline is a leader in suicide prevention that serves as a free, confidential crisis hotline that is available to all individuals 24/7. This lifeline will connect callers to the nearest crisis center, which will provide counseling and mental health referrals. You can chat live with a certified professional here, or you can reach them by phone at 1-800-273-TALK(8255). Individuals who are deaf, hard of hearing or have hearing loss can contact the lifeline via TTY at 1-800-799-4889.
  • SAMHSA's National Helpline is a confidential, free helpline that is available to all individuals 24/7. It provides free referrals to local treatment facilities, support groups and community-based organization for those with psychiatric problems and/or substance abuse disorders. National representatives speak in both English and Spanish. You can reach them by phone at 1-800-622-HELP(4357). Individuals who are deaf, hard of hearing or have hearing loss can contact the helpline via TTY at 1-800-487-4889.
  • SAMHSA's Disaster Distress Helpline is a confidential national hotline dedicated to providing immediate crisis counseling for individuals experiencing acute emotional distress, including anxiety and depression, related to any natural or human-caused disaster, including tornadoes, hurricanes, earthquakes and mass violence. A toll-free, multilingual helpline, its support services are available to all residents within the US and its territories. You can reach them by phone at 1-800-985-5990.
  • Crisis Textline is a free, confidential service for individuals within the US, Canada and UK, in which a live, trained Crisis Counselor responds to your thoughts and concerns regarding any difficult situation you are experiencing, including suicidal thoughts, acute stress, anxiety, depression, attempted self-harm, bullying and emotional abuse. Your text will always be read, interpreted and answered by a empathetic, supportive individual who has been trained by Crisis Textline, and is committed to ensuring that you remain safe.
  • NAMI's Text Helpline can be accessed by texting 'NAMI' to 741741, is free, confidential and available 24/7 and offers critical information on what to do in an emergency or crisis, information on mental health crisis services and preparations that can be taken for a potential crisis. Helpline volunteers provide meaningful, empathetic support, connect callers with their local NAMI crisis center and provide information about NAMI Educational Programs and Support Groups.
  • The NJ Disaster Mental Health Helpline provides public access to targeted mental health services in case of a natural or man-made disaster or terrorist attack. It is used to support the services of NJ’s Disaster and Terrorism Branch in times of declared disasters or incidents that significantly disturb NJ’s communities. You can dial in to this lifeline at 1-877-294-HELP (4357). Individuals who are deaf, hard of hearing or have hearing loss can contact the lifeline via TTY at 877-294-4356. Hours of operation are Monday through Friday from 8:00AM to 8:00PM EST.
  • NJ Mental Health Cares is New Jersey’s behavioral health information and referral service. Their staff of behavioral care specialists use their empathy, personal experiences and understanding to provide emotional support for situations involving a mental health crisis. You can dial in to this lifeline at 1-866-202-HELP (4357), or reach them via email at help@njmentalhealthcares.org. Individuals who are deaf, hard of hearing or have hearing loss can contact the lifeline via TTY at 973-870-0677. Hours of operation are 7 days a week, from 8:00AM to 8:00PM EST.
  • NJ Hopeline offers help from trained specialists that are available 24/7 to provide confidential telephone counseling, support and referrals to local resources in order to help you navigate through a mental health crisis. You can dial in to this counseling helpline at 1-855-654-6735. Besides reaching out to a specialist via phone, NJ Hopeline also offers web-based support services through a peer support live chat. You can control your anonymity through your user profile.
